About

Introduction and background.

I was born on August 23, 2004, in Istanbul. I completed my primary and middle school education in Şişli, and later continued my high school years at TED Istanbul. About a year before the pandemic, while studying at TED Istanbul, I became interested in game development and software. I started learning basic programming languages such as C# and Java—object-oriented languages that are relatively easy to pick up. However, during the pandemic, I discovered game development itself and realized how much I enjoyed creating games. I downloaded beginner-friendly game engines like Unity and spent the entire lockdown experimenting and learning. During my final year of high school, I was a bit unsure about what field I wanted to pursue as a career. Eventually, I learned that Bahçeşehir University had a Digital Game Design department focused entirely on developing video games. After earning an ApplyBAU scholarship through the university entrance exam, I began my studies in this program in 2023 as a first-year student. I am currently in my third year, second semester. Throughout this period, I attended many GDC-related events this year, met numerous people in the industry, and developed several games both individually and with teammates—and I continue to do so. Thanks to the diverse coursework I’ve taken, I’ve also learned how to use interfaces of various modeling tools and audio applications, even if they're outside my main discipline. With the guidance of my instructors, I’ve gained a deep understanding of many aspects of design and game design principles. I’ve conducted research, produced projects, and continued developing my skills. I've learned a great deal about game development, and I am still learning while gaining hands-on experience along the way.
